How to Create a QR Code

This short guide explains how to make useful QR codes for websites, text, email, phone numbers, and WiFi. Use the QR Maker tool to generate and download QR images instantly.

Step-by-step (basic)

  1. Open the QR Maker tool.
  2. Choose the QR type (URL, Text, Phone, Email, WiFi).
  3. Enter the content — e.g., a full URL including https://.
  4. Pick a size (256 or 512 recommended for print).
  5. Click Generate and then Download (PNG).

Best practices

  • Include full URLs: Use complete links (with https) so scanners open the right page.
  • Test before printing: Scan with multiple phone apps to confirm the destination.
  • Choose the right size: For business cards, keep QR at least 2 cm square. For posters, make it larger.
  • Provide context: Add short text (e.g., “Scan to view menu”) above or below the QR code so users know what to expect.
  • Avoid encoding too much data: Very dense QR codes require larger print sizes to be readable.

Common QR types

URL: Direct visitors to a web page. Best for promotions and links.

Text: Small notes, instructions, or short messages.

Phone: Click-to-call numbers for businesses or support lines.

Email: Pre-filled email templates (mailto:) for feedback or contact.

WiFi: Save and share WiFi credentials (see the WiFi page for details).

Troubleshooting

  • If scans fail, try a higher-size PNG (512) or print at a larger size.
  • Make sure no important content is outside the quiet zone (the blank border around the QR).
  • Use high-contrast colors (black QR on white background works best).
